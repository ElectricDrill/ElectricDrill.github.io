ğŸ” Features Breakdown

Attributes

Attributes are the fundamental numerical values that define an entity's capabilities. In most RPGs they are: strength, dexterity, intelligence, constitution, etc. However, you define your own attributes. You choose the names and the min and max values (if any).

Then you can group them in attribute sets. A easily sharable object that can be reused across different entities.

Apply flat or percentage modifiers to your entities' attributes.

Statistics

Statistics are the derived, in-game values that directly impact gameplay (such as Physical Attack, Armor, or Crit Chance). As for attributes, you can define your own and group them in statistic sets to be assigned to entities. Such stat sets can also be modular, for example you can define the a stat set for the movement statistic, one for dealing damage, and one for receiving damage. You can then define sets on the base of them basing on the entity's needs, so that you assign only the relevant stats to each entity.

Statistics can scale with attributes. Define how each statistic scales on the base of one or more attributes.

As for attributes, statistics can have lower and upper bounds.

Statistics can also have flat and percentage modifiers. Moreover, they support the powerful stat-to-stat modifiers. They allow you to create buffs/debuffs that change a statistic basing on the value of another statistic.

Experience & Levels

Define your own level progression curves, and the experience required to reach each level.

If an entity doesn't level up, you can set fixed base stats and attributes.

Classes & Progression

Through classes (such as warrior, mage, rogue, etc.), you can define unique attributes and statistics progression paths. Assign progression curves for each attribute and statistic to each class, to give them the unique flavor and playstyle.
